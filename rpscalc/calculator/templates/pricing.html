    Cost cap and pricing

    The level of success the RPS policy you have just created will not only depend on the trajectory and technologies selected, but the cost of renewable energy and the RPS cost cap. Many states include cost caps in their RPS policies to protect electricity customers from large rate increases.

    For some states, this cost cap will limit the amount of renewable power purchased under the RPS. On this page you can establish your own cost cap as well as adjust the default assumptions about the cost of renewable power.
