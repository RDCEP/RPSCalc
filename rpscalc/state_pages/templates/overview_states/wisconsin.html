Wisconsin's Renewable Portfolio Standard (RPS) establishes a minimum amount of renewable energy that must be sold by the state's electricity providers. This page identifies and describes the features of the RPS and how they work together to define the minimum amount of renewable energy that will be sold in Wisconsin.

    {% endblock %} {% block state_statute %}

    Wisconsin enacted Act 9 in 1999; the first state to introduce a RPS without having restructured its electric-utility industry. Originally the RPS required 2.2% of electricity sold to be from renewables by 2012. In 2006, the goal was increased to 10% by 2015. Providers can petition the Public Service Commission to extend the compliance deadline.
