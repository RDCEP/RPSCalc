Wisonsin’s Renewable Portfolio Standard (RPS) is intended to increase use of renewable resources in the state by establishing the minimum amount of renewable energy that the state’s electricity providers must sell in a given year. This legally-established renewable energy procurement goal gradually increases over time to meet the overall target of 15% renewable power by 2025.

    Wisconsin enacted Act 9 in 1999; the first state to introduce a RPS without having restructured its electric-utility industry. Originally the RPS required 2.2% of electricity sold to be from renewables by 2012. In 2006, the goal was increased to 10% by 2015. Providers can petition the Public Service Commission to extend the compliance deadline.
