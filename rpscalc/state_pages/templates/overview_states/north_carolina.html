North Carolina's Renewable Portfolio Standard (RPS) establishes a minimum amount of renewable energy that must be sold by the state's electricity providers. This page identifies and describes the features of the RPS and how they work together to define the minimum amount of renewable energy that will be sold in North Carolina.

{% endblock %} {% block state_overview %}
  • 12.5% of the state's electricity supplied from Investor-Owned Utilities must be from renewable sources by 2021 and 10% of the state's electricity supplied from electric cooperatives and municipal utilities must come from renewable sources by 2018.
  • Carve-outs for solar, swine waste, and poultry waste.
  • There is not explicit cap on a change in retail electricity pricing.

    The RPS includes a requirement for 0.2% of the RPS source from solar by 2018, and 0.2% from swine waste also by 2018. Another 9,000 MWh of electricity is required to be sources from poultry waste by 2014.

    The RPS was established in 2007 with Senate Bill 3, which required all investor-owned utilities to supply 12.5% of the state's retail electricity sales with qualifying renewable energy sources by 2021. The bill also sets out that municipal utilities and electric cooperatives supply 10% of their sales with renewable sources by 2018. The North Carolina Utilities Commission enforces the requirements, which serve the purpose of developing electricity procurement plans for renewable power for the state. Renewable energy may be bundled with RECs.
